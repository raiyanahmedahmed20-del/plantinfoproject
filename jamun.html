Jamun

About the Jamun Tree

Scientific Name: Syzygium cumini

Common Names: Jamun, Java Plum, Indian Blackberry

Family: Myrtaceae

Native Region: Indian Subcontinent and Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Evergreen fruit tree

Fruit Color: Dark purple to black

Uses: Fruit consumption, traditional medicine, juices, jams, desserts

Details: Jamun is a popular tropical fruit known for its sweet and slightly tangy flavor. The fruit has a deep purple color and contains many nutrients that are beneficial for health.

Jamun fruits are rich in iron, vitamin C, antioxidants, and fiber. They are commonly eaten fresh and are also used to make juices, syrups, ice creams, and jams.

In traditional medicine, different parts of the Jamun tree such as seeds, bark, and leaves are used for various remedies. Jamun seeds are especially known for helping manage blood sugar levels.

The Jamun tree grows tall and provides excellent shade. It is commonly planted in gardens, roadsides, and parks because of its dense green foliage and nutritious fruits.
