Ashoka Tree

About the Ashoka Tree

Scientific Name: Saraca asoca

Common Names: Ashoka, Sorrowless Tree

Family: Fabaceae

Native Region: India and Southeast Asia

Average Height: 6–10 meters

Flower Color: Orange, red, and yellow

Uses: Ornamental, medicinal, cultural and religious importance

Details: The Ashoka tree is a beautiful evergreen tree known for its fragrant and colorful flowers. It is widely planted in gardens, temples, and parks because of its attractive appearance.

The tree blooms during spring and produces clusters of orange and red flowers that gradually turn yellow. Its dense green leaves and elegant shape make it a popular ornamental plant.

In Ayurveda, the bark and flowers of the Ashoka tree are used in traditional medicine for treating various health conditions, especially related to women's health.

The Ashoka tree is considered sacred in Indian culture and is often associated with peace, love, and prosperity. It is also mentioned in many ancient texts and stories.
