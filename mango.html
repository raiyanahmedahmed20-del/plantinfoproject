Mango

About the Mango Tree

Scientific Name: Mangifera indica

Common Names: Mango, King of Fruits

Family: Anacardiaceae

Native Region: South Asia

Plant Type: Large evergreen fruit tree

Fruit Color: Green, yellow, orange, or red

Uses: Fruit consumption, juices, desserts, pickles, wood, traditional medicine

Details: Mango is one of the most popular tropical fruits in the world and is often called the "King of Fruits" because of its delicious taste and rich nutritional value.

Mango fruits are juicy, sweet, and rich in vitamins A and C, fiber, and antioxidants. They are eaten fresh and also used in juices, shakes, ice creams, desserts, candies, and pickles.

The mango tree is a long-living evergreen tree that can grow very tall and provide dense shade. It blooms with small fragrant flowers before producing fruits during the summer season.

In Indian culture, mango leaves are considered sacred and are often used in festivals, decorations, and religious ceremonies. The wood of the mango tree is also used for furniture and other wooden items.
