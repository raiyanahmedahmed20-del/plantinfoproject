Moringa

About the Moringa Tree

Scientific Name: Moringa oleifera

Common Names: Moringa, Drumstick Tree, Miracle Tree

Family: Moringaceae

Native Region: India

Plant Type: Fast-growing deciduous tree

Uses: Nutrition, medicine, cooking, herbal products

Details: Moringa is a highly nutritious plant widely valued for its leaves, pods, flowers, and seeds. It is commonly known as the Drumstick Tree because of its long edible seed pods.

The leaves of the moringa tree are rich in vitamins, minerals, protein, calcium, iron, and antioxidants. They are often used in soups, curries, salads, and health supplements.

In traditional medicine, moringa is used to support immunity, improve digestion, and promote overall health. Many parts of the tree are used in Ayurvedic and herbal treatments.

Moringa grows quickly in warm climates and can survive in dry conditions. Because of its nutritional value and multiple uses, it is considered one of the most useful trees in the world.
