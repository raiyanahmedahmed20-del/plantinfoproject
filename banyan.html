Banyan Tree

About the Banyan Tree

Scientific Name: Ficus benghalensis

Common Names: Banyan, Indian Banyan

Family: Moraceae

Native Region: Indian Subcontinent

Average Lifespan: Hundreds of years

Height: Up to 20–30 meters

Uses: Shade, traditional medicine, ornamental tree, environmental protection

Details: The Banyan tree is one of the largest and oldest trees in the world. It is famous for its aerial roots that grow downward from branches and develop into additional trunks. This allows the tree to spread over a very large area.

Banyan trees provide shelter to birds, animals, and humans. They are commonly planted near temples, parks, and village centers because of their huge canopy and cooling shade.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, different parts of the tree such as bark, roots, leaves, and latex are used for treating various ailments.

The Banyan tree is also considered the national tree of India and symbolizes immortality, strength, and wisdom.
