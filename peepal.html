Peepal

About the Peepal Tree

Scientific Name: Ficus religiosa

Common Names: Peepal, Sacred Fig, Bodhi Tree

Family: Moraceae

Native Region: Indian Subcontinent and Southeast Asia

Plant Type: Large deciduous tree

Uses: Religious purposes, traditional medicine, shade, environmental protection

Details: The Peepal tree is one of the most sacred trees in India and is often found near temples, monasteries, and holy places. It is known for its heart-shaped leaves and long lifespan.

In Buddhism, the Peepal tree is especially important because Gautama Buddha is believed to have attained enlightenment under a Bodhi tree, which is a type of Peepal tree.

The tree provides excellent shade and supports many birds and animals. It is also valued for its role in improving air quality and maintaining ecological balance.

In traditional Ayurvedic medicine, the bark, leaves, fruits, and roots of the Peepal tree are used for treating various health conditions. The tree symbolizes wisdom, spirituality, and peace.
